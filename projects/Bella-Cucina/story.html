Where It Began

A Family Recipe,
A Chicago Dream

Bella Cucina was born in 2009 from a simple, fierce conviction — that the food of Southern Italy, specifically the bold, rustic, sun-soaked traditions of Calabria and Sicily, deserved a permanent home in Chicago.

Marco Esposito grew up in a small village outside Cosenza, where Sunday meant the whole family gathered around his Nonna Rosa's table. The pasta was always hand-rolled. The tomatoes always came from her garden. The wine was always poured generously.

In 2003, Marco moved to Chicago to pursue a culinary education. But nothing he found quite captured the spirit of his grandmother's cooking. So in 2009, with his wife Sofia and a collection of handwritten recipe cards, he opened Bella Cucina on a quiet corner of downtown Chicago.

Fifteen years later, the family has grown — in the restaurant and beyond. But the philosophy has never changed: every dish must taste like it was made by someone who loves you.

What We Believe

Our Philosophy

"Food is not fuel. It is memory, culture, love, and community — served on a plate."

🌿

Tradition First

We don't reinvent Southern Italian cuisine — we honour it. Our recipes are rooted in generations of Calabrian and Sicilian tradition, adapted only where the finest local ingredients demand it.

🫱

Made by Hand

Every pasta shape, every sauce, every bread in this kitchen is made by hand, every day. There are no shortcuts. There is no substitute for a cook who cares about what they're making.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑

Family Atmosphere

Whether you're celebrating a birthday, a first date, or simply Tuesday — you are a guest in our home. We treat every table with the same warmth we'd give our own family.

🌾

Honest Ingredients

We partner with local farms for seasonal produce and import only the DOP ingredients that can't be replicated — San Marzano tomatoes, Parmigiano Reggiano, Sicilian olive oil, and more.

🌍

Sustainability

We reduce food waste through nose-to-tail cooking philosophy, compost all kitchen waste, source from sustainable fisheries, and run our dining room using renewable energy.

🎓

Growing Our Team

We invest heavily in our people. Every cook in this kitchen receives ongoing training, mentorship, and fair pay. Many of our senior staff started as apprentices and have grown with us for over a decade.

The Chef Behind the Kitchen

Marco Esposito

Founder · Head Chef · Calabrian Native

Marco grew up in a small village in the Calabrian mountains, where food was not a luxury but a language. His grandmother Rosa taught him to make pasta at age seven, his grandfather taught him to tend a garden, and the village taught him that the best meals are the ones that take all day to prepare and last all evening to eat.

After studying at the Culinary Institute of Milan and spending three formative years in Naples and Palermo, Marco moved to Chicago in 2003. He spent six years in some of Chicago's finest kitchens before opening Bella Cucina in 2009 — the restaurant he always wished he could find in America.

Today, Marco personally oversees every menu, trains every cook, and still rolls pasta by hand every Tuesday and Friday morning.

Our Journey

Fifteen Years of
Good Food & Stories

2009
Bella Cucina Opens Its Doors

Marco and Sofia Esposito open Bella Cucina on a quiet corner of downtown Chicago with 40 seats, a wood-fired oven, and Nonna Rosa's handwritten recipe book. The first review calls it "Chicago's most soulful Italian table."

🍝
📰
2011
Chicago Tribune "Best New Restaurant"

After two years of growing word-of-mouth, the Chicago Tribune names Bella Cucina one of the city's best new restaurants, describing the rigatoni alla 'nduja as "the most authentic Southern Italian dish in Chicago."

2013
Expansion — New Dining Room & Bar

Growing demand leads to a full expansion — the restaurant doubles in size to 90 seats, a full Italian wine bar is added, and the catering program launches. The family also hires its first dedicated pastry chef, Chef Lucia Romano.

🏗️
🏆
2018
Marco Named "Best Italian Chef" in Chicago

Chicago Magazine recognizes Marco with its Best Italian Chef award, citing his unwavering dedication to Calabrian technique and his passionate mentorship of young cooks. The catering arm now serves 100+ events per year.

2021
James Beard Award Nomination

Marco is nominated for a James Beard Award in the "Best Chef: Great Lakes" category — a milestone for a self-described family cook who simply wanted to serve Nonna Rosa's food to Chicago. The restaurant celebrates with a special tasting menu.

⭐
🎂
2024
15th Anniversary — Back to Our Roots

To celebrate 15 years, Bella Cucina introduces a special "Nonna Rosa Menu" featuring the original 12 dishes from the 2009 opening night — a tribute to the woman whose handwritten recipes started it all. The occasion brings Nonna Rosa herself to Chicago for the first time.

What We Use

Ingredients with
a Story

We believe food tastes better when you know where it comes from. Every week we source from the same trusted producers we've worked with for over a decade — local when possible, Italian when irreplaceable.

  • 🇮🇹
    San Marzano DOP Tomatoes

    Grown in the volcanic soil of the Agro Sarnese-Nocerino valley, imported from our partners in Campania every season.

  • 🇮🇹
    Parmigiano Reggiano DOP (30-month)

    From a small family-run caseificio in Reggio Emilia. The same producer since our second year of business.

  • 🇺🇸
    Seasonal Produce — Local Illinois Farms

    We partner with three Illinois farms for seasonal vegetables, micro-herbs, and heirloom tomatoes. The menu changes with the harvest.

  • 🇮🇹
    Sicilian Extra-Virgin Olive Oil

    Cold-pressed Nocellara del Belice from a small estate outside Trapani. We use it as both a cooking medium and a finishing oil.
