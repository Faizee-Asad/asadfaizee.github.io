Process & Timeline
6 questions

We follow a structured 4-step process: 1) Discovery & Strategy — we understand your goals and requirements. 2) UX & Wireframing — we map structure before design. 3) UI Design & Prototype — high-fidelity designs approved by you. 4) Development & Launch — clean code, tested and deployed. You approve each stage before we proceed.

Timelines vary by scope: a simple website takes 2–4 weeks; a web application or e-commerce store takes 4–8 weeks; a full ERP or enterprise system takes 3–6 months. We provide a detailed timeline estimate before starting — and we stick to it. We'll always communicate proactively if anything changes.

Just a conversation. Tell us what you want to build, who your users are, and what problem you're solving. We'll ask the right questions and handle all the technical scoping. You don't need to provide a specification document — though if you have one, we're happy to review it.

We need your input at key approval stages — after wireframes, after designs, and before launch. Outside of those checkpoints, we handle everything independently. Most clients spend 2–4 hours per week reviewing and giving feedback. We never leave you guessing — weekly progress updates are standard.

Yes, within reason. Each stage includes a round of revisions. Changes requested within the agreed scope are included. Significant scope additions may require a change order with adjusted timeline and cost — but we'll always discuss this transparently before proceeding. We never surprise you with hidden charges.

Yes. We sign NDAs for all projects where confidential business information is involved. We also provide a formal project agreement covering scope, timeline, payment terms, and IP ownership before any work begins. Your business ideas and data are safe with us.

Pricing & Payment
5 questions

Costs vary widely depending on scope and complexity. As a rough guide: a simple informational website starts at ₹15,000–₹30,000; a web application or e-commerce store typically ranges from ₹50,000–₹2,00,000; a full enterprise ERP system starts at ₹2,00,000+. We provide a detailed, itemised quote after our discovery call — no vague ballparks.

For fixed-price projects we typically structure payments as: 40% upfront (project kick-off), 40% at mid-project milestone (designs approved / development half-complete), and 20% on final delivery. For ongoing retainers, we invoice monthly. We're flexible and happy to discuss structures that work for your cash flow.

We primarily offer fixed-price project quotes for well-defined scopes — so you always know exactly what you're paying. For ongoing work, maintenance retainers, or projects with evolving requirements, we can work on a monthly retainer or time-and-materials basis. We'll recommend the right model for your situation.

No. Our quotes are transparent and itemised. Any third-party costs (domain, hosting, premium plugins, API subscriptions) are clearly called out separately. If scope changes during the project, we discuss and agree on any cost implications before proceeding. We don't believe in surprise invoices.

Yes, we do consider discounts for early-stage startups, educational institutions, and registered NGOs on a case-by-case basis. If this applies to you, mention it when you reach out and we'll see what we can do. We believe good software should be accessible to organisations doing meaningful work.

Support & Maintenance
4 questions

Every project includes a 30-day free support period after launch — covering any bugs, fixes, or minor adjustments. After that, we offer monthly maintenance retainers that cover security updates, performance monitoring, content changes, and priority bug fixes. We don't abandon projects after delivery.

Yes. We take on maintenance and support contracts for existing websites and applications, regardless of who originally built them. We'll conduct a code audit first to understand the codebase, flag any issues, and then agree on a support scope that makes sense for your needs.

For clients on a maintenance retainer, we respond to critical issues within 4 hours and non-critical requests within 1 business day. For one-off support requests without a retainer, response time is typically 1–2 business days. We're reachable by phone, WhatsApp, and email.

Yes. For all CMS-based projects (WordPress, custom admin panels), we provide a handover training session — either in person in Malegaon or via video call. We also provide written documentation and, where applicable, short video walkthroughs so your team can manage content independently without needing to call us for every update.
